\section{Overview of the Five Epoch Habitability Maps}
\label{sec:results_overview}

The Bayesian framework produces posterior mean habitability maps
$P(H \mid \featurevec)$ at each of the five temporal anchor epochs and,
via the multi-epoch temporal reconstruction (Section~\ref{sec:multi_epoch}),
at 72 intermediate epochs spanning $-3.8$ to $+6.5\gya$.
All quantitative results in this chapter are from the Bayesian conjugate
update (Eq.~\ref{eq:posterior_mean}), with posterior mean bounded in
$[P_\text{min}, P_\text{max}] = [0.150, 0.696]$ (Section~\ref{sec:bounds}).
A Random Forest classifier is used solely to derive permutation feature
importances (Section~\ref{sec:importances}); its probability outputs are
not used as scientific results.
Table~\ref{tab:epoch_summary} summarises the Bayesian posterior statistics
for the five anchor epochs.

The qualitative ranking Kraken $>$ Belet $>$ Selk is preserved across all
nine parameter variants in Table~\ref{tab:sensitivity}, and the maximum
absolute change in any site's $P(H)$ is $\pm0.07$ relative to baseline ---
well below the 0.19-unit gap between Kraken and Selk.  The $w_1$ sensitivity
(panel c) is most policy-relevant: even if the liquid-hydrocarbon weight were
reduced from 0.25 to 0.15, polar lake shores would still rank above equatorial
land sites.  The framework's qualitative conclusions are therefore robust to
the hyperparameter choices.

The global prior mean $\mu_0$ rises from $0.29$ at the PAST epoch
(reflecting less organic accumulation and an impact-melt-dominated
habitability model) to $0.33$ for the PRESENT and near-future epochs,
then to $0.67$ at the FUTURE epoch when the water--ammonia ocean priors
dominate.  The posterior range $[P_\text{min}, P_\text{max}]$ tracks
$\mu_0$ because $\alpha_0 = \mu_0 \kappa$ scales both bounds.
The PAST $\mu_0$ is slightly lower than PRESENT because the impact-melt
and cryovolcanic features carry high individual priors but the
established eight features are set conservatively to reflect
early-Titan conditions (less organic accumulation, uncertain methane cycle).

The PRESENT and NEAR\_FUTURE posteriors are near-identical ($\mu_0 = 0.331$
in both cases, spatial correlation $r > 0.999$), confirming the negligible
effect of the $\sim$1.4\% luminosity increase over 250\,Myr
(Section~\ref{sec:epoch_nf}).  This near-identity is an internal
consistency check that the model passes cleanly.

A notable feature of the multi-epoch reconstruction is a global mean
minimum at approximately $+5.0\gya$.  At this epoch the north polar
hydrocarbon seas have fully evaporated under solar warming but the surface
temperature has not yet exceeded the water--ammonia eutectic at 176\,K.
\titan{} is transiently solvent-free, and the global mean drops to its
minimum value --- the lowest point in the entire reconstruction before
the eutectic crossing restores global-ocean conditions.  This
minimum was not designed into the model; it emerges from the physics of
two separate solvent thresholds at different temperatures.

\section{Present-Epoch Habitability}
\label{sec:results_present}

\begin{quote}\small
\textit{Note on scope.} The maps in this chapter quantify
\textbf{present-day solvent habitability} --- the capacity of each
location to support liquid-solvent chemistry at the Cassini epoch.
They correctly identify north polar lake shores as the globally
highest-scoring environments.  The \dragonfly{} mission targets
\textbf{prebiotic chemistry habitability} (past liquid-water contact),
for which impact craters are the primary targets.
The relationship between these two frameworks is discussed in
Section~\ref{sec:two_frameworks}; readers are encouraged to
consult that section before interpreting equatorial site scores.
\end{quote}

\subsection{Global Spatial Structure}
\label{sec:present_spatial}

The present-epoch habitability map is dominated by a pronounced
north--south asymmetry driven by the distribution of liquid hydrocarbon.
The highest posterior probabilities form a crescent-shaped band of elevated
habitability at 60--85\textdegree{}N, coinciding with the margins of the
three major north polar seas.  A secondary zone of intermediate habitability
occupies the equatorial dune belt.  South of $\sim$30\textdegree{}S the map
shows uniformly moderate-to-low values, with a small enhancement around
Ontario Lacus at 72\textdegree{}S.

\subsection{North Polar Lake Margins: The Highest-Scoring Environments}
\label{sec:present_polar}

Shoreline pixels along the southern margin of Kraken Mare and the eastern
margin of Ligeia Mare are the highest-scoring environments in the
present-epoch map, reaching $P(H) \approx 0.44$ (Kraken south shore)
and $0.43$ (Ligeia eastern margin).  These values are close to
$P_\text{max} = 0.696$ when all features are maximised, but the
shoreline weighting reflects the combination of maximum $f_1 = 1.0$
with elevated $f_4$ (methane cycle), $f_5$ (surface--atmosphere
interaction), and $f_7$ (geomorphological diversity) that characterise
the lake-margin environment.  Counterintuitively, the \textit{open
sea surfaces} score \textit{below}
the adjacent shorelines despite possessing maximum liquid hydrocarbon
($f_1 = 1.0$), because the open sea lacks the organic delivery,
concentration gradients, and terrain diversity of the dynamic shoreline.
This contrast is the most physically meaningful result in the present-epoch map.

The physical mechanism driving the shoreline enhancement involves three
coupled processes:

\paragraph{Continuous organic delivery via the channel network.}
The fluvial channel network \citep{Miller2021channels} terminates
preferentially at the lake margins, with channel densities of
0.2--0.5 channels per kilometre along the lacustrine margin compared to
$<$0.05 channels per kilometre in the mid-latitude interior.  Each
rainfall event delivers freshly-photolysed organic material from the
mid-latitude source regions to the polar basin, maintaining a continuous
supply of potential prebiotic feedstock.  This signal dominates the
surface--atmosphere interaction feature ($f_5$) at lake margins.

\paragraph{Chemical concentration gradients from evaporation.}
\titan{}'s sea levels vary on the 29.5-year Saturnian seasonal timescale
as methane preferentially evaporates during northern summer \citep{Hayes2016}.
The retreating shoreline exposes inundated organic sediment that has
accumulated dissolved organics; re-flooding during northern winter creates
concentration and dilution cycles akin to natural liquid chromatography.
Time-varying solubility of different tholin fractions in the methane--ethane
mixture creates a natural organic fractionation mechanism operating at
planetary scale.

\paragraph{The chemical interface between two regimes.}
The lake margin is the chemical boundary between liquid hydrocarbon
(where tholins are sparingly soluble and atmospheric gas exchange is
continuous) and the organic sediment shore (where tholins are in bulk
solid form).  The geomorphological diversity feature ($f_7$) is
elevated here precisely because the terrain class transitions from
lake to plains or dune within the averaging window.

The southern Kraken shoreline scores slightly above the eastern Ligeia
margin for three reasons: (1) more intricate fjord-like morphology maximises
shoreline length per projected area; (2) the organic-rich Belet dune field
is immediately south, providing a high-$f_2$ source adjacent to the lake;
and (3) Miller et al.\ (2021) show the highest channel densities draining
into the southern Kraken basin.

\subsection{Equatorial Dune Belt: Organic Richness Without Solvent}
\label{sec:present_dunes}

The equatorial dune seas (Shangri-La, Belet, Aztlan, Fensal) form a
secondary zone with $P(H) \approx 0.32$--0.35.  The dominant driver is
organic abundance: dune terrain receives the highest TERRAIN\_ORGANIC score
(0.82), confirmed by VIMS spectroscopy as tholin-dominated
\citep{LeMouelic2019}.  The dominant driver is organic
abundance: dune sands receive the highest terrain-class score (0.82),
confirmed by VIMS spectroscopy as tholin-dominated \citep{LeMouelic2019}.
Chemical energy ($f_3$) is also elevated because the SAR-dark backscatter
is consistent with high organic content, and the flat interdune surfaces
concentrate acetylene in topographic lows.

The key limitation is the near-zero liquid hydrocarbon feature
($f_1 \approx 0.02$) in the dune interior.  Episodic methane rainfall
has been directly observed in equatorial regions \citep{Turtle2011},
but the liquid evaporates within days to weeks, insufficient to sustain
the persistent chemical environment that the model treats as the primary
present-epoch habitability prerequisite.

\subsection{Low-Habitability Regions}
\label{sec:present_low}

The Xanadu region (90--150\textdegree{}W, equatorial) scores
$P(H) \approx 0.24$, comparable to Selk crater.  Its water-ice-rich
composition assigns the mountain terrain class ($f_2 = 0.25$) to much of
its interior --- a 3--4$\times$ reduction in organic abundance relative to
adjacent dune terrain.  Its water-ice-rich composition assigns the mountain
terrain class (score 0.25) to much of its interior --- a 3--4$\times$
reduction in $f_2$ relative to adjacent dune terrain.  Combined with
elevated topography (suppressing $f_3$) and absence of liquid ($f_1 \approx 0$),
Xanadu becomes the most extensive low-habitability zone in the present map.
Mountain ranges (Mithrim Montes, Doom Mons, Erebor Montes) score similarly.
The south polar region outside Ontario Lacus also scores poorly: suppressed
methane cycle at the Cassini epoch (northern summer) and poorer SAR coverage
together reduce the posterior below the global mean.

\section{Past-Epoch Habitability: Impact-Melt Chemistry}
\label{sec:results_past}

\subsection{Geographic Pattern}
\label{sec:past_spatial}

The PAST epoch map is qualitatively distinct from the PRESENT.  The north
polar enhancement disappears entirely ($s_1 = 0.10$; no polar seas).  In
its place, a spatially clustered pattern emerges centred on confirmed impact
structures: the impact-melt proximity feature creates localised enhancements
of 0.05--0.15 above regional background at each major crater.  The equatorial
dune belt provides a moderate-score background (organic abundance at
$\sim$40\% of present-day levels), but impact sites clearly dominate the
geographic structure.

\paragraph{Physical logic.}
At $-3.5\gya$ the sole source of liquid water on \titan{} was episodic
post-impact melt.  The impactor flux during the LHB was 100--1000 times the
present background rate \citep{OBrien2005}, producing approximately one
significant melt event per 1000 years somewhere on the surface.  These ponds
--- $\sim$50--100\,km$^2$ area, $\sim$50--100\,m depth --- persisted for
$10^3$--$10^4$ years before refreezing, during which all the precursors for
Strecker amino acid synthesis (Equation~\ref{eq:strecker}) were simultaneously
present: liquid water--ammonia solvent from the melt, aldehydes from tholin
decomposition, and HCN from the nitrogen-rich atmosphere.

Menrva (392\,km diameter, $P(H)_\text{past} = 0.37$) leads the crater
ranking because its exceptional diameter implies a proportionally larger
melt volume and longer liquid persistence: \citet{OBrien2005} calculate that
a 392\,km transient crater excavates $\sim$10$^5$\,km$^3$ of crust, producing
a melt pond that remains liquid for $\sim$10$^4$\,yr.  Selk (90\,km,
$P(H)_\text{past} = 0.31$) ranks fourth despite its smaller size because it
sits at the Shangri-La dune--Adiri plains boundary, placing the transient
impact melt in contact with the most organic-rich terrain class on \titan{}.
Cryovolcanic candidates (Hotei Regio, Sotra Facula) lead the overall PAST
ranking because the surface--atmosphere interaction prior $\mu_5 = 0.50$ at
that epoch rewards potential cryolava conduits above crater melt ponds ---
a model prediction that would be tested by identifying cryovolcanic signatures
in any future spectral survey from the LHB era.

At the PRESENT epoch, all crater floor scores drop to near the prior mean
(Table~\ref{tab:craters}, $P(H)_\text{pres.} \approx 0.24$--0.28) because
the impact-melt proxy feature is inactive and the liquid-hydrocarbon feature
is near zero at equatorial latitudes.  This contrast directly encodes the
central two-framework argument: craters score high in the past (transiently
habitable impact melts) but low in the present (no liquid remaining).

\paragraph{Energetics of melt generation.}
For a 50\,km diameter rocky impactor at \titan{}'s escape velocity of
2.64\,km\,s$^{-1}$:
\begin{equation}
  E_k = \tfrac{1}{2} m v^2 \approx
  \tfrac{1}{2} \cdot \tfrac{4}{3}\pi(25{,}000)^3 \cdot 3000
  \cdot (2640)^2 \approx 2.6\times10^{24}\,\text{J}
  \label{eq:impact_energy}
\end{equation}
Approximately 10\% of this energy melts ice rather than vaporising or
ejecting material \citep{OBrien2005}, giving $\sim2.6\times10^{23}$\,J.
At the heat of fusion of water ice ($3.34\times10^5$\,J\,kg$^{-1}$),
this melts $\sim7.8\times10^{17}$\,kg $\approx 800$\,km$^3$ of ice,
forming a pond of $\sim$1000\,km$^2$ at $\sim$50--80\,m mean depth.
For the Selk-scale impactor ($D \approx 40$\,km), the melt volume is
$\sim$50--100\,km$^3$.

\paragraph{Strecker synthesis conditions.}
\citet{Madan2026} demonstrated that the LHB-era melt ponds on \titan{}
simultaneously satisfy all kinetic and thermodynamic conditions for the
Strecker reaction: temperature 200--350\,K (within the reaction's accessible
range), weakly acidic to neutral pH (from dissolved \ce{CO2} and organic
acids), \ce{NH3} from the water--ammonia melt, \ce{HCN} from the nitrogen-rich
atmosphere ($\sim$100\,ppb stratospheric abundance; \citealt{Vuitton2007}),
and aldehyde precursors from thermal decomposition of tholins in the hot
ejecta.  The estimated amino acid yield of $\sim$0.1--1.0\% by dissolved
organic mass gives $\sim10^{12}$--$10^{13}$\,g of amino acids per major
impact --- comparable to the amino acid delivery from carbonaceous chondrites
on early Earth \citep{Pizzarello2006}.

\section{Lake-Formation Transition: $-1.0$ to $-0.5$\,Gya}
\label{sec:results_lf}

The LAKE\_FORMATION anchor at $-1.0\gya$ captures \titan{} as the polar
lake system begins to consolidate.  The prior mean $\mu_0 = 0.331$ is identical to PRESENT, reflecting
the same eight features and prior specification.  The spatial distribution
differs from PRESENT because the liquid hydrocarbon scale is only 10\%
of present, and the impact-melt proximity feature still contributes
a residual crater-site signal.  Comparing this
map to PRESENT reveals three changes: (1) the north polar lake margin signal
begins to emerge above background; (2) the impact-melt crater signal has
largely faded to background; (3) the cryovolcanic flux feature, still
moderately elevated relative to the present, provides residual local
enhancements at candidate cryovolcanic sites.

The transition from $-1.0$ to $-0.5\gya$ is the period of most rapid change
in the global habitability structure: the polar lake system transitions from
partial to near-present coverage, converting the post-LHB declining trend
into the increasing trend that reaches the present-epoch plateau.  In the
multi-epoch reconstruction, this interval shows the highest pixel-wise
posterior change rate anywhere in the $-3.8$ to $+4.0\gya$ range.

\section{Near-Future Epoch: The Stable Plateau}
\label{sec:results_nf}

The NEAR\_FUTURE epoch at $+0.25\gya$ confirms the expected near-identity
with the PRESENT.  The $\sim$2.5\% luminosity increase corresponds to a
surface temperature rise of $\sim$1--2\,K, entirely absorbed by the prior
and producing no spatially structured change in any feature.  \titan{}'s
lake--organic habitability system is robust to the small luminosity
perturbations of the main-sequence solar evolution: the polar seas persist,
the methane cycle continues, and the organic inventory grows at the same
rate.  The NEAR\_FUTURE and PRESENT maps are functionally equivalent
habitability states.  Figure~\ref{fig:near_future_global} confirms this
visually: the spatial correlation between the NEAR\_FUTURE and PRESENT
posteriors exceeds $r = 0.999$.

\section{Future-Epoch Habitability: The Red-Giant Ocean}
\label{sec:results_future}

\subsection{The Thermal Transition}
\label{sec:future_thermal}

The FUTURE anchor at $+5.9\gya$ is the last epoch at which the solar model
gives $T_s > 176$\,K.  At this epoch, $T_s \approx 466$\,K --- far exceeding
the water--ammonia eutectic at all latitudes.  The global water--ammonia ocean
is unambiguously present.  The preceding eutectic crossing at $+5.1\gya$ and
the subsequent luminosity collapse at $+6.0\gya$ bracket an ocean window
of $\sim$0.8\,Gyr.

The FUTURE posterior has global mean 0.69 with $\sim$49\% of pixels at or
above $P(H) = 0.75$.  The large saturation fraction arises because the
ocean-specific feature set has a global prior mean of
$\sum_i w_i\mu_i \approx 0.80$, mapping to $P(H) \approx 0.73$ for an
average pixel.  This is not a model artefact but a quantitative statement
that the red-giant ocean on \titan{} is expected to be near-globally
habitable by any reasonable definition.

\subsection{Why Future Titan is Exceptional}
\label{sec:future_exceptional}

Three factors combine to make the red-giant \titan{} ocean window potentially
the most favourable prebiotic chemistry environment in the post-main-sequence
solar system.

\paragraph{The organic stockpile.}
At the present photochemical deposition rate of
$5\times10^{-14}$\,g\,cm$^{-2}$\,s$^{-1}$ \citep{Lavvas2011}, the
surface accumulates $\sim$63\,g\,cm$^{-2}$\,Gyr$^{-1}$.  Over
$\sim$16\,Gyr from the present to $+5.9\gya$, the organic layer grows
by $\sim$10--36\,km depth (depending on compaction).  Tholin hydrolysis
experiments demonstrate that wetting this material with liquid water at
273--373\,K produces amino acids, nucleobase analogues, and sugar-like
polyols at 0.1--5\% by mass \citep{Neish2009}.  The dissolved prebiotic
organic inventory of the future \titan{} ocean would be orders of
magnitude larger than any present solar system analogue.

\paragraph{Bioavailable nitrogen.}
The water--ammonia ocean provides dissolved \ce{NH3} as a direct and abundant
nitrogen source, removing the nitrogen-fixation bottleneck that limited
prebiotic chemistry on early Earth.  Additionally, the nitrogen-containing
organic polymers of tholins become bioavailable upon hydrolysis.

\paragraph{Duration.}
The $\sim$0.8\,Gyr ocean window is comparable to the $\sim$300\,Myr between
the first evidence for life on Earth ($\sim$3.8\,Gya) and the establishment
of the last universal common ancestor (LUCA, $\sim$3.5\,Gya).  Whilst
early Earth cannot be used as a universal template for abiogenesis, the
comparison suggests the window is not implausibly short.

\subsection{Spatial Structure of the Future Posterior}
\label{sec:future_spatial}

Whilst near-globally elevated, the FUTURE map retains meaningful structure.
The deepest topographic basins in the GTDE --- overwhelmingly the former
polar sea depressions (Kraken, $\sim$2--3\,km below the geoid; Ligeia,
$\sim$1--2\,km) --- will become the deepest parts of the ocean, where
hydrostatic pressure is highest, dissolved organic chemistry is most
concentrated, and bottom-water circulation driven by thermal convection
is strongest.  These basins are expected to score $P(H) \approx 0.75$--0.80
under the FUTURE prior set (all eight features elevated;
Eq.~\ref{eq:posterior_mean}).  The current topographic
highlands (Xanadu, equatorial mountain chains, southern highland terrane)
will become islands or shallow banks with reduced liquid and thermal
features, scoring $P(H) \approx 0.55$--0.65 --- still well above
any present-epoch non-polar environment, but lower than the deep basins.

\section{Temporal Evolution of Titan's Habitability}
\label{sec:temporal_evolution}

\subsection{Three Regime Changes}
\label{sec:three_regimes}

The multi-epoch reconstruction reveals three qualitatively distinct
habitability regimes across geological time, each dominated by a different
solvent and surface process:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Impact-chemistry era} ($-3.8$ to $-1.5\gya$):
    crater-site-dominated, driven by the impact-melt proximity feature
    ($w = 0.10$, $\mu = 0.50$).  Site scores at confirmed craters
    exceed $P(H) \approx 0.35$; background equatorial terrain scores
    near the prior mean.  Organic accumulation builds slowly over this
    period.

  \item \textbf{Lake-chemistry era} ($-1.0\gya$ to $+4.0\gya$):
    north polar lake-margin-dominated.  The prior mean $\mu_0 = 0.33$
    is stable throughout; the spatial contrast between lake margins
    ($P(H) \approx 0.44$) and dry equatorial terrain ($P(H) \approx 0.28$)
    reaches its maximum at the present epoch.  This regime persists
    for $\sim$4.5\,Gyr.

  \item \textbf{Global ocean era} ($+5.1$ to $+6.0\gya$):
    near-globally elevated; the FUTURE prior mean $\mu_0 = 0.67$ with
    hard maximum $P_\text{max} = 0.85$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:bounds})
    governs the posterior range.
\end{enumerate}

The transition from regime 1 to regime 2 (at $\sim$$-1.0$ to $-0.5\gya$)
is marked by the north polar mean ($>$60\textdegree{}N) overtaking the
crater-site mean at $\sim$$-1.5\gya$.  The transition from regime 2 to
regime 3 is marked by the global mean overtaking the north polar mean at
$\sim$$+5.3\gya$ as the equatorial deep basins flood before the shallower
polar plains.  These transitions are quantified in
Figure~\ref{fig:temporal_trend}.

\subsection{The Habitability Minimum at $+5.0$\,Gya}
\label{sec:minimum}

The most significant novel prediction of the multi-epoch reconstruction is
the habitability minimum at $\sim$$+5.0\gya$.  As solar warming evaporates
the polar seas (complete by $+5.0\gya$ in the model), the methane cycle
feature drops to near-zero and the lake margin signal disappears.  The
surface is warmer than at the present epoch ($T_s \approx 140$\,K) but
has no stable solvent.  The global mean drops from 0.53 at the present
to $\approx$0.35 at the minimum --- a 34\% decrease.  The minimum is
transient: $\sim$0.1\,Gyr later the eutectic is crossed and the ocean
begins to form.  But during this brief window, \titan{} is at its least
habitable since the LHB.

\section{Selk Crater: Detailed Analysis}
\label{sec:selk}

\subsection{Geological and Observational Context}
\label{sec:selk_context}

Selk crater (199\textdegree{}W, 7\textdegree{}N; $\approx$90\,km diameter;
\citealt{Hedgepeth2020}) occupies the geological boundary between the
Shangri-La dune field (west) and the Adiri bright plains (east).  Its SAR
morphology shows a radar-bright outer annulus and a radar-dark interior
floor \citep{Wood2010}: the bright annulus is most consistently interpreted
as the crystallised front of an ancient water--ammonia impact melt that
spread outward from the basin.  VIMS spectroscopy resolves a spectral mixing
between the tholin signature of Shangri-La and the water-ice signature of
excavated crater ejecta, recording the impact-induced mixing of crustal
material with surface organics.

\subsection{The Dragonfly Traverse}
\label{sec:selk_traverse}

The planned traverse from the Belet dune field through the crater ejecta
to the crater floor follows an increasing geodiversity gradient:
from homogeneous dune terrain ($f_7 \approx 0.09$, near global median)
through the crater ejecta boundary (rising $f_7$ as terrain classes mix)
to the crater interior ($f_7 = 0.629$, 7.8$\times$ the global median,
highest of any equatorial site).
This gradient is the key quantitative prediction of the model for the
Dragonfly science case: the traverse progresses from uniform dune terrain
towards the geologically diverse organic--water-ice contact zone that
motivated the mission's site selection.

\section{Other Sites of Astrobiological Interest}
\label{sec:other_sites}

\subsection{Kraken Mare Southern Shoreline}
\label{sec:kraken}

The southern shoreline of Kraken Mare (310\textdegree{}W,
65--75\textdegree{}N) is the highest-scoring environment in the present-epoch
model at $P(H) \approx 0.44$ (Table~\ref{tab:top10_present}, rank~1).  Three
physically distinct processes concentrate at this shoreline simultaneously:
(1)~confirmed surface liquid ($f_1 = 1.0$) supplies a non-aqueous solvent at
all times; (2)~the channel network terminating at the lake margin
($f_5 \approx 0.65$) continuously delivers concentrated organic substrate from
the Belet dune field immediately to the south; and (3)~the fjord-like coastal
morphology of the Kraken southern shore creates the highest shoreline
geodiversity ($f_7 \approx 0.76$) of any polar lake, encoding the intersection
of at least three terrain classes within 45\,km.  The southern margin's slight
advantage over the Kraken northern shore (rank~3, $P(H) = 0.43$) reflects this
more intricate coastline geometry.

This site --- the highest-scoring present-day location in the entire analysis
--- is not included in the \dragonfly{} baseline mission, and constitutes the
primary quantitative recommendation for a second-generation \titan{} surface
mission targeting present-day solvent habitability.

\subsection{Ligeia Mare Eastern Shoreline}
\label{sec:ligeia}

Ligeia's eastern shoreline (115--130\textdegree{}W, 74--78\textdegree{}N)
scores $P(H) \approx 0.43$ (Table~\ref{tab:top10_present}, rank~2).
The slight margin behind Kraken reflects the adjacent terrain: the eastern
Ligeia margin borders the Dilmun plains (organic abundance score 0.68) rather
than the organic-richer Belet dune belt (0.82) that flanks Kraken's southern
shore.  Ligeia is more methane-dominated than Kraken in composition
\citep{Mastrogiuseppe2014}, suggesting a different shoreline chemistry that
the model does not resolve at the feature level but which would produce
distinct DraMS signatures in a future mission.  The overlap of the 95\%
HDI intervals of Kraken and Ligeia (Figure~\ref{fig:hdi_comparison}) means
the posterior means are not statistically distinguishable at current prior
specification --- both represent equivalent-quality targets.

\subsection{Ontario Lacus: The Southern Analogue}
\label{sec:ontario}

Ontario Lacus (179\textdegree{}W, 72\textdegree{}S) scores
$P(H) \approx 0.44$ --- below the north polar seas but above the equatorial
interior.  Three factors explain the gap: Ontario is $\sim$27$\times$ smaller
than Kraken ($\sim$15{,}000 vs $\sim$400{,}000\,km$^2$); the methane cycle
is suppressed at the south pole at the Cassini epoch (northern summer); and
the lake is actively shrinking at the Cassini epoch \citep{Turtle2011b},
reducing the stable shoreline signal.

\subsection{Huygens Landing Site: Ground-Truth Comparison}
\label{sec:huygens}

The \huygens{} probe landing site (192.3\textdegree{}W, 10.6\textdegree{}S)
scores $P(H) \approx 0.35$ --- a moderate southern plains
site without liquid.  The GCMS detected toluene, benzene, cyanogen, and
acetonitrile at this location \citep{Niemann2005}, confirming the presence
of complex organics consistent with the model's moderate organic abundance
score ($f_2 \approx 0.54$, Adiri plains class) and moderate chemical energy
($f_3 \approx 0.38$).  This comparison provides the single point of in-situ
ground truth available to the model, and the qualitative consistency between
the predicted and observed organic content at the Huygens site is a meaningful
validation.

\subsection{FUTURE Epoch ($+5.9$\,Gya): Global Ocean}
\label{sec:top10_future}

By $+5.9\,\gya$ the water--ammonia eutectic has been exceeded for $\sim$800\,Myr and a global ocean covers the surface.  The entire feature set changes: $f_{\mathrm{aq}}$ (water--ammonia solvent), $f_{\mathrm{org}}$ (organic stockpile), and $f_{\mathrm{dis}}$ (dissolved chemical energy) replace the present-epoch features.  Former lake basins score highest because they are deepest (more dissolved organics and thermal stratification), whilst equatorial dune fields rank immediately behind (the accumulated organic substrate is now fully dissolved in warm water-ammonia).

\titan{} at the present epoch is distinguished from all other candidates by
combining a confirmed cycling surface solvent with a very large organic
